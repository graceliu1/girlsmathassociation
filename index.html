Promoting Interest in Mathematics

 providing opportunities for girls to improve their math abilities
The Girls Math Association (GMA), is a 100% student-run club that aims to provide support to girls interested in mathematics and STEM. The club strives to create an environment where girls can their interests in mathematics through a variety of resources including competitions, tutoring, and interactive activities. 
​
Since mathematics is closely connected to science and engineering, it is critical for those who plan to pursue a STEM career path to have a sufficient background. We need to start encouraging more girls to expand their interests!

Improving & Collaborating

--Purpose--

Students work together during club meetings to prepare for math competitions like the AMC 10/12 and Purple Comet Math Meet! Among others, these math competitions promote important logical reasoning and problem-solving skills. The club also provides information and practice sessions for the math section of the SAT and SAT II. 

--Progress--

As of the 2020-2021 school year, we are planning to add collaborative tutoring options for students who need help with regular math classes at school. Tutoring will be offered from Algebra I through AP Statistics and AP Calculus AB. Members are able to earn volunteer hours by supporting the club and tutoring other students. ​

--Planning--

Our future aspirations include hosting a math competition for girls across the Tri-Valley and participating in more math competitions as a club.  Events scheduled for the 2020-2021 school year include: Who Wants to Be a Mathematician?; MathCON; and Mathleague. We are also working on reaching out to more students at our school and beyond!

2020 Math Contest

In August 2020, the Girls Math Association is hosting our first ever 2020 GMA Math Contest! This is an opportunity for girls in grades 5 through 8 to heighten their interest in STEM fields. The contest will be held online. For more information and registration details, please check out our Math Contest page!

About Us

We are a club from Dublin High School composed of students from grades 9-12. We have been active since 2018. Our club advisor is Allison Malone, a math teacher at Dublin High School. To read our Mission Statement or learn about our officers and history, check out our About Us page!
